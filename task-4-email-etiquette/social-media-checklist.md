# Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist

As a Student Digital Ambassador, maintaining a responsible and professional online presence is essential. Use this checklist as a guide for safe and effective social media use.

## ✅ The Do's (Responsible Practices)
1. **Enable Privacy Settings:** Regularly review your account settings to control who can see your personal posts and information.
2. **Think Before You Post:** Always consider if a post or comment could negatively impact your future academic or professional opportunities.
3. **Fact-Check Information:** Verify the source and accuracy of news or academic alerts before sharing them with your peers.
4. **Maintain Professionalism:** Keep your LinkedIn and other professional profiles updated with your current degree and institution details.
5. **Engage Positively:** Use your platform to share helpful resources and engage in constructive discussions with your college community.

## ❌ The Don'ts (Risky Behaviors)
1. **Share Sensitive Data:** Never post your home address, phone number, or one-time passwords (OTPs) on public platforms.
2. **Post Offensive Content:** Avoid sharing material that is discriminatory, hateful, or unprofessional, as it can lead to serious consequences.
3. **Engage with Cyberbullies:** Do not respond to "trolls" or participate in online harassment; instead, report and block such accounts.
4. **Tag Without Permission:** Respect the privacy of your classmates and faculty by asking before tagging them in photos or videos.
5. **Use Unprofessional Language:** Avoid using slang or "text-speak" when interacting with organizations or official college accounts online.
