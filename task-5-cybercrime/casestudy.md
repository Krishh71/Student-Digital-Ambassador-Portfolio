## Part A: UPI & Bank Fraud Case Study
*Based on recent trends in Indian Cyber Law: [Source Link](https://www.scconline.com/blog/post/2026/01/24/real-life-cybercrimes-india-cases-remedies-prevention/)*

### What exactly is this crime?
This is basically "Digital Robbery." Scammers use a mix of trickery and technical loopholes to get into your UPI apps or bank accounts and drain your balance before you even realize what's happening.

### How it happens (The Step-by-Step Trap)
1. **The Call or Text:** It usually starts with a random call. Someone might claim to be from your bank, or they might pretend to be a "buyer" interested in something you’re selling on OLX.
2. **The Panic Move:** They try to scare you by saying your account is about to be blocked, or they try to excite you by saying you’ve won a cashback prize.
3. **The Trick:** This is where most people get caught. They send you a UPI "Request" link. They tell you that clicking it and entering your PIN will *receive* the money. In reality, entering your PIN *sends* your money to them.
4. **The Cleanup:** Once they have your PIN or gain access to your phone via a screen-sharing app (like AnyDesk), they empty your account and move the money through several different banks to make it impossible to track.

### Who is at risk?
Honestly, everyone, but students and elderly people are targeted most often. Students are usually targeted through fake job offers or "easy money" schemes.

### The Impact
The obvious hit is losing your hard-earned money. Beyond that, it causes a lot of mental stress and can lead to your personal ID being used for other illegal activities.
