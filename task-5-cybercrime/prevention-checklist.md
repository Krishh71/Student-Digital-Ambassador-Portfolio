# "Stay Safe Online" Checklist 🛡️

### 💸 Financial & UPI Safety 
1. **The Golden Rule of UPI:** You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone tells you to enter your PIN to get a "refund" or "prize," they are 100% trying to scam you.
2. **Scanner Caution:** Before scanning a QR code in a shop or sent over WhatsApp, double-check the name that pops up on your screen. Scammers often stick fake QR codes over real ones.

### 📱 Social Media & Privacy
3. **Double-Lock Your Accounts:** Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for WhatsApp, Instagram, and Gmail. Even if someone steals your password, they can't get in without that second code.
4. **The "Stranger Danger" Filter:** Don’t accept follow requests or messages from random accounts offering "Easy Part-time Jobs" or "Investment Tips." These are usually hooks for phishing.
5. **Think Before You Post:** Avoid posting photos of your ID cards, hall tickets, or hostel room numbers. Small details are all a stalker or hacker needs to find you.

### 🌐 Browsing & Downloads
6. **Spot the "Look-alike" Sites:** Scammers make fake VIT or bank login pages. Always check the URL spelling—if it says `vltbhopal` instead of `vitbhopal`, close the tab immediately.
7. **Avoid "Free" Screen-Sharing:** Never download apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer because a "customer care" person told you to. They will be able to see your OTPs and private chats in real-time.
8. **Public Wi-Fi Risk:** Never log into your bank account or use UPI while connected to free college cafe or airport Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data for anything involving money.

---

### 🚨 How to Report a Crime
If you or a friend gets scammed, don't be embarrassed—act fast! The first two hours are the "Golden Hour" to freeze the stolen money.

* **Call the Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately to report financial fraud.
* **Official Portal:** File a formal complaint at **[www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)**.

**Stay Alert, Stay Safe!**
